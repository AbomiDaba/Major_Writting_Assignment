Curatorial Statment

Systemic inequality has plagued our country since any one can remember. It is like a cancer that has to be weeded out. This concept tells people they are not good enough or born the right way to the point they can not get what is due to them because they are not like the majority. So, this exhibition will deal with different types of systemic issues that people deal with primarily in the Unites States. I present artists with different backgrounds to express their ideas and solutions to problems with inequality.

The artists I have chosen in my exhibition all have unique approaches and styles of art, yet they have the same end goal; creating a better tomorrow for all people. With the United States continually becoming more and more diverse, especially compared to its inception, the idea of a fairer society is imperative to living in a more fulfilling and free country. I present a wide spectrum of injustices that continue to rear its ugly head into our nation. From health care, gender, race, equity, voting, and so on we’ll deal with how these different issues can be protested and dealt with, even with just art. Protesting and doing your part may look different, maybe even unorthodox, but any effort is a step closer to a better tomorrow for all people.

This is very important to address because with diversity increasing in so many ways, we have to continue the fight for equality. Some may think because we’ve come so far that we don’t have to be as active in making changes for the better. Or even the only way to make a change is to be a politician, but that couldn’t be further from the truth. Some may think art is just a talent or skill used for entertainment. Well, these artists I have here will debunk that faulty ideology and hopefully show how versatile methods to making a difference can be. Some of these artists grew up in third world countries in impoverished environments yet used are to make actual changes in societies such as Edwardo Kobra and Ben Shahn just to name a couple. So my hope is to encourage my audience to keep the fight alive and find ways in which you can contribute to continuing the effort for a more equal country for all.
